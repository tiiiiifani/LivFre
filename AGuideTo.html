A Guide to the Human Body

William Chen

The human body is known to be the most complex of all species. The following texts is simply giving a broad idea of what part of the human anatomy to focus on, when looking at a healthy and well structured body. Hope you’ll have fun exploring and getting to know more about your body.

When it comes to health or getting healthy people tend to think it’s the end of the world, like starting now you have to eat these nasty tasting vegetables, oatmeal, and got to stop having sugar. Before we bust some myths about those typical mindset let look at the basic need of your body. Your body is made up of minerals, proteins, fats, and water. Minerals play a role in developing a strong bone structure in your body as well as transmitting nerve impulses. Protein help keep your muscles and organ healthy by repairing it if damaged, it is also a source of energy, and a type of building block for your body. Fats often seen as a bad thing even though it is an essential need in your body, fats help protein do their job by acting like a messenger believe it or not, fats also protect your vital organs. Lastly water is needed in your body as your body is composed about 60% of water, water also has impact on circulation, digestion, maintaining a healthy body temperature, and much more.

Minerals, Protein, and water is understandable of why they are important. But why fats? Isn’t fats what ‘causes hearts disease, cardiovascular issues, clogging up our blood stream, and so on? To break down that question, consuming fats have two categories and both deals with cholesterol. Cholesterol is a waxy substance that may affect the natural flow of the bloodstream. LDL cholesterol known as low density lipoprotein which is the bad cholesterol that having too much can increase the risk of heart problems, and ‘causing blockage in the bloodstream. Then there’s HDL cholesterol high density lipoprotein that helps prevent/lower risk of heart problems, and blockage in the bloodstream. LDL cholesterol are often found in fatty foods that are made up of trans fat and saturated fat. Trans fats are industrial made fats that goes through a process of hydrogenation, basically preventing food from rancid. Saturated fats are often found in dairy product like coconut oil, milk, cheese, etc. Although saturated fats increases LDL cholesterol consuming just a bit of it won’t hurt you. The healthy fats that increase more HDL cholesterol in the body are known as polyunsaturated fats and monounsaturated fats. Polyunsaturated fats and monounsaturated fats are both found in vegetable oil, olive oil, peanut oil, canola oil, etc. The difference between saturated and unsaturated fats is basically the amount of hydrogen atoms in the chain of carbon atoms. Hydrogen and carbon atoms are a compound found in fats, basically the lesser hydrogen atoms in the chain of carbon atoms make it easier to break down.

That was about all the basics needed to understand how the body function. To end it in a more intriguing way we will go over some myths about healthy food. First the mindset of putting food in the category of healthy and unhealthy is bad. Love what you eat just in moderation. It’s better to look at food in a way of if it’s nutritional or not than healthy or not. Vegetable is really important in a good diet, but that does not mean eat the vegetable steam or raw. Add flavor into it, make it taste better, make it edible for you. It doesn’t hurt to add salt or pepper in it, it definitely does not hurt to mix it with meat. Sugar is bad for you! Nope, sugar can be addictive but it is not bad for you. Like everything else have it in moderate consumption. Sugar does not really make you fat, what make you fat is really your habit. Do you sleep enough? Are you active? Are you giving the nutrition your body seek for? There’s a lot of cards in your hand when it comes to deciding what make you fat/unhealthy. And sugar itself is not to be blamed entirely.
